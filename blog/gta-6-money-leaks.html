What the April 2026 Rockstar Hack Actually Revealed

In April 2026, the hacking group ShinyHunters breached Rockstar's cloud infrastructure and threatened to release data unless a ransom was paid. Rockstar refused. The data dropped.

Here's what the hack actually contained — and what it didn't:

Confirmed

GTA Online generates $1.3M per day

The leaked financial data confirmed GTA Online's daily revenue figures — around $1.3 million per day as of early 2026. Over $5 billion total since 2013. Shark Cards account for 74% of revenue. [Source: RockstarINTEL]

BS

The hack revealed GTA 6 Online features

False. Rockstar confirmed the breach was limited to "non-material company information" — financial analytics, contracts, marketing plans. No GTA 6 gameplay assets, money methods, or development roadmap was leaked. Any claim of GTA 6 content attributed to this hack is fabricated.

Visible in Trailer 2. Directly relevant to money methods — harder wanted level escapes mean higher risk for certain high-reward activities. [Source: Rockstar Games]

How to Spot a Fake GTA 6 Leak

With the game launching November 19, fake leaks will intensify. Five checks before believing anything:

  • Where did it first appear? Real leaks surface on GTAForums or r/GamingLeaksAndRumours before spreading to YouTube. If you can't trace the original post, it's suspect.
  • Who verified it? Journalists like Jason Schreier (Bloomberg) and Tom Henderson have verified GTA 6 information in the past. Unknown accounts with no track record are not credible sources.
  • Does the graphics quality match? Real leaked footage typically looks rough — GTA 6's 2022 leak was clearly early development footage. Polished "leaks" are almost always AI-generated or mods.
  • Does it contain specific numbers? No verified leak has ever contained specific GTA 6 Online payout figures. Any claim with exact dollar amounts is made up.
  • Has Rockstar responded? Rockstar confirmed the 2022 Lapsus$ hack within hours. They confirmed the April 2026 breach. When real leaks happen, Rockstar responds. Silence usually means nothing was actually stolen.
